How It Works, How to Pay, and How to Get Your Tax Deduction

Welcome

Thank you for being part of 100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley. Since 2013, our members have donated over $757,000 to local charities — all by showing up (or tuning in) four times a year and contributing to the organizations that inspire them. This guide covers everything you need to know as a member.

How It Works

The concept is beautifully simple:

  • We meet once per quarter at a location in the Wood River Valley (with a virtual option when possible). Meetings last about one hour.
  • Members nominate local charities. Three finalists present their mission for ten minutes each (including Q&A).
  • Each member decides how to allocate their contribution across the three presenting organizations. You can give your entire minimum $100 to one organization, split it equally among all three, or allocate it in any proportion you choose.
  • There is no voting and no "winning" organization — all three charities benefit from our collective giving based on how members direct their contributions.
  • With 100 members each contributing a minimum of $100, that can mean $10,000 or more in immediate, unrestricted funding distributed among the three presenting organizations.

That's it. No fundraising galas, no silent auctions, no committees to join. One hour, four times a year, minimum $400 total.

Note: The minimum contribution is $100 per quarter, and many active members choose to give more. You're welcome to contribute above the minimum if you're able.

What You'll Receive from the Leadership Board

Our Communications Coordinator handles all member communications. Here's what to expect:

Before Each Meeting

Meeting announcement email (at least two weeks before) with the date, time, location (including virtual access link if available), and the names and descriptions of the three presenting charities. You'll also get a reminder email a few days before the meeting.

After Each Meeting

Results and payment email (within 48 hours) with the names and missions of the three presenting charities, plus all available payment options for each organization — website link, Zelle info, Venmo profile, ACH details, DAF grant instructions, QCD information, and mailing address for checks. Everything you need to make your contribution will be in this one email.

Two Weeks Later

A gentle follow-up reminder if you haven't contributed yet. No judgment — just a nudge.

Website

Meeting dates, past presenting organizations, the nomination form, and Leadership Board contact info are always available at www.100menwhocarewoodrivervalley.com.

Check Handling Procedure

If you prefer to contribute by check but can't deliver it directly to the charity, you can give it to the Communications Coordinator for delivery. Here's how we handle checks to ensure security and accountability:

  • Make your check payable directly to the charity (not to 100 Men Who Care or any individual)
  • Write "100 Men Who Care" in the memo line
  • The Coordinator will photograph each check for records
  • A "VOID — COPY FOR RECORDS" watermark is applied to the digital copy
  • Original checks are hand-delivered to charities within 7 days
  • You'll receive confirmation when your check has been delivered

Three Important Rules About Payments

1. Always pay the charity directly.

Never send your contribution to another member, a personal Venmo account, or any intermediary (with the exception of hand-delivering checks to the Communications Coordinator as described above). Your payment must go directly to the charity's official account. This is what makes your donation tax-deductible and keeps the Group's model clean and simple.

2. Verify you're paying the right account.

If using Venmo, look for the blue checkmark on the charity's profile — this means they're a verified nonprofit. If using Zelle or ACH, use only the details provided in the Leadership Board's post-meeting email. If something looks off, ask before you send.

3. Identify your payment.

Always include "100MWC" or "100 Men Who Care" in the memo, note, or comment field. This helps the charity track our contributions and makes it easier for them to acknowledge us as a group.

Your Tax Deduction

Because you contribute directly to 501(c)(3) charities, your donations are tax-deductible. Here's what you need to know:

Automatic Receipts

  • Online donations, Venmo, and PayPal: You'll usually receive an automatic email receipt. Save it for your records.
  • DAF grants: Your DAF sponsor (Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, etc.) will provide documentation of the grant.

Manual Receipts

  • Zelle and ACH: These services do not automatically generate tax receipts. Email the charity after your payment and request a written acknowledgment. Your bank statement is helpful backup but is not sufficient on its own for IRS purposes.
  • Check: Keep a copy of the check or your bank's cleared-check image. You may also request a receipt from the charity.
  • QCD: Your IRA custodian will provide documentation. QCD amounts are excluded from your taxable income (different from a deduction, but equally valuable for tax purposes).

IRS Rule

For any single donation of $250 or more, the IRS requires a written acknowledgment from the charity. Since our minimum contribution is $100, this typically does not apply unless you choose to give more. However, if you make a larger contribution, be sure to obtain written confirmation.

What If You Can't Attend a Meeting?

Life happens. Many of our members contribute regularly without attending meetings. If you miss a meeting, you're still expected to contribute your minimum $100. After the presentations, you'll receive the results email with the three presenting charities' names and all payment options. Simply review the information and allocate your contribution among the organizations that resonate with you.

If you're going to miss multiple meetings in a row, just let the Leadership Board know. We'd rather you stay connected than drop out quietly.

Nominating a Charity

Any member in good standing can nominate a nonprofit. To nominate:

  • Download the Nomination Form from our website or request one from the Leadership Board.
  • Complete the form with the charity's name, mission, 501(c)(3) status, and how the funds would be used.
  • Submit it to the Leadership Board before the deadline (typically two weeks before the meeting).
  • If your charity is selected as one of the three finalists, you (or a representative of the charity) will give a ten-minute presentation at the meeting (including Q&A).
Tip: The best presentations help members understand the organization's mission, the community it serves, and why the work matters. A specific project ask is not required — focus on telling the story of impact.

Staying in Good Standing

A member is in good standing if they've contributed to at least three of the last four quarterly meetings. Good standing matters because only members in good standing can nominate charities. Everyone can attend meetings regardless of standing.

The Leadership Board tracks contributions by checking with the presenting charities after each meeting to confirm approximate totals and participation.
