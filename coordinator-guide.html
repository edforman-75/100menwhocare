Communications Coordinator Guide

100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley
Operational Handbook

Welcome to your role as Communications Coordinator. This guide covers all your operational responsibilities, from managing charity nominations to coordinating post-meeting communications. Your role is essential to keeping our members informed and our operations running smoothly.

Time Commitment: Approximately 3–5 hours per quarter, concentrated around meeting weeks. Most tasks can be done on your own schedule.

Quarterly Cycle Overview

4 Weeks Before Meeting

  • Open nomination window
  • Send nomination reminder to members
  • Coordinate with Chair on venue and meeting logistics

2 Weeks Before Meeting

  • Close nominations
  • Chair selects three finalists
  • Send Charity Payment Info Form to three finalists
  • Send meeting announcement email to all members
  • Update website with meeting details

1 Week Before Meeting

  • Follow up with finalists to ensure Payment Info Forms are completed
  • Configure payment page with all three charities
  • Test payment page functionality
  • Send meeting reminder email

Day of Meeting

  • Display payment page QR code during presentations
  • Track attendance (optional)
  • Note any member feedback or questions

Within 48 Hours After Meeting

  • Send post-meeting email to all members with three charities and payment details
  • Send notification email to each of the three charities with matching list information
  • Update website with meeting results

2 Weeks After Meeting

  • Send gentle reminder email to members
  • Begin tracking contribution confirmations (if using payment page)

3–4 Weeks After Meeting

  • Contact charities to confirm approximate participation and total contributions
  • Compile impact report for members and website
  • Archive records for the quarter

Managing Charity Nominations

Nomination Form Management

  • Nomination forms should be available on the website year-round
  • You can use Google Forms, Typeform, or embed directly on the website
  • Required fields:
    • Nominating member name and contact info
    • Charity legal name and EIN
    • Mission statement and community served
    • Why this charity needs support
    • Contact person at the charity

When You Receive a Nomination

  1. Send confirmation email to nominating member
  2. Forward to Chair for review and selection
  3. If selected as finalist, notify both the member and the charity
  4. Send Charity Payment Info Form to finalist (see next section)

💡 Tip: Use a simple Google Form or Typeform for payment info collection. Send it immediately when the charity is selected as a finalist, with a clear deadline (e.g., "Please complete by [date] so we can have your info ready for the meeting").
⚠️ Equal Treatment: All three charities must receive identical treatment in all communications and on the payment page. No charity should be featured more prominently than another. Presentation order should be rotated or randomized.

Payment Page Configuration

The payment page should be live before the meeting and remain active for at least 30 days after.

What Goes on the Payment Page

  • Equal presentation of all three charities side-by-side
  • Each charity's logo, name, and one-sentence mission
  • All available payment methods for each charity with clear labeling:
    • Fee-Free Methods (check, Zelle, ACH, DAF, QCD) — highlighted with green badge
    • Methods with Fees (credit card, Venmo, PayPal) — noted with fee percentage
  • Links to online donation pages
  • Instructions for QCD and DAF giving
  • Optional: Contribution confirmation form (to help with matching list)

QR Code for Meeting

  • Generate a QR code linking to the payment page
  • Display it during the meeting (projected on screen or printed)
  • Members can scan and access payment details immediately

Check Handling Procedure

Some members prefer to give you checks for delivery to the charities. Here's the secure handling process:

When You Receive a Check

  • Verify check is made out to the charity (NOT to you or 100MWC)
  • Verify "100 Men Who Care" is in the memo line
  • Photograph both sides of the check
  • Apply "VOID — COPY FOR RECORDS" watermark to digital copy
  • Store digital copy securely (encrypted folder or password-protected)
  • Log the check in your tracking spreadsheet:
    • Member name
    • Charity
    • Amount
    • Check number
    • Date received
    • Date delivered
  • Hand-deliver original check to charity within 7 days
  • Get delivery confirmation (photo of handoff, email confirmation, or receipt)
  • Send confirmation email to member: "Your check to [Charity] has been delivered"
  • Retain digital copies for one year, then delete
⚠️ Security: Never deposit checks yourself. Never endorse checks. Checks must go directly to the charity's account. Your role is courier only.

QCD/DAF Matching List

When members use Donor Advised Funds (DAF) or Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCD), the payment comes from an institutional account like "Fidelity Charitable" or "Charles Schwab" rather than the member's name. The matching list helps charities connect these gifts back to the member.

How to Create the Matching List

  1. If using payment page with confirmation form: Export submissions filtered by payment method = "DAF" or "QCD"
  2. For each charity, compile a list of:
    • Member name
    • Institution/Custodian (e.g., "Fidelity Charitable", "Schwab", "Vanguard")
    • Approximate send date
    • Amount (if member provided it)
  3. Send to charity contact person with subject: "100MWC - Matching List for Institutional Gifts"

Email Template to Charity

Subject: 100MWC - Matching List for Institutional Gifts Hi [Contact Name], Several of our members are contributing to [Charity Name] via Donor Advised Fund (DAF) grants or Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCD) from their IRAs. These gifts will arrive from institutional accounts (like "Fidelity Charitable" or "Charles Schwab") rather than the member's personal name. The list below will help you match these gifts to our members: EXPECTED INSTITUTIONAL GIFTS: • John Smith - via Fidelity Charitable - sent approx. [date] • Jane Doe - via Schwab Charitable - sent approx. [date] • Bob Jones - via Vanguard (QCD) - sent approx. [date] Please send personalized acknowledgment letters/emails to each member at the addresses below when their gift arrives: [Member contact details] Questions? Let me know. Thank you! [Your Name]

Impact Reporting

3–4 weeks after each meeting, contact each charity to confirm participation and totals.

What to Ask Each Charity

  • How many individual gifts were received from 100MWC members?
  • What was the approximate total? (If they're comfortable sharing)
  • Any feedback about the experience?

Compile and Share

  • Update website with meeting results
  • Send impact summary to members

  • Charity payment info forms

Recommended Tools

  • Google Drive - Shared folder with Chair for nominations, payment info, templates
  • Google Sheets - Member roster, check log, impact tracking
  • Google Forms - Nomination form, charity payment info form, optional payment confirmation
  • Mailchimp or similar - Email campaigns (free tier sufficient)
