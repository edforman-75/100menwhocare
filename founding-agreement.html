FOUNDING AGREEMENT

100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley
An Unincorporated Charitable Giving Association
Established 2013 • Wood River Valley, Idaho
100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley ("the Group") is a voluntary, unincorporated association of individuals who share a commitment to supporting charitable organizations in the Wood River Valley of Idaho. Founded in 2013 by Marty Lyon, the Group is inspired by the principle that collective generosity, when focused and coordinated, can create meaningful impact in our community.

This Founding Agreement establishes the operating principles of the Group. It is not a legal contract and creates no binding obligations. It is intended to set clear expectations so the Group can operate transparently, effectively, and with continuity across changes in leadership.

"Never doubt that a small group of thoughtful committed individuals can change the world; indeed, it's the only thing that ever has." — Margaret Mead

Article I — Name and Purpose

  • Name. The name of this association is 100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley.
  • Purpose. The purpose of the Group is to bring together individuals who each contribute directly to local nonprofit organizations. Three organizations present at each quarterly meeting, and each member directs their contribution across one, two, or all three presenting organizations as they choose, thereby amplifying the impact of individual generosity.
  • No Treasury. The Group does not collect, hold, invest, or distribute funds. Members make their charitable contributions directly to the presenting organizations. The Group has no bank account and no financial assets.

Article II — Membership

  • Eligibility. Membership is open to any individual who shares the Group's charitable mission and agrees to abide by this Founding Agreement.
  • Commitment. Each member commits to contributing a minimum of $100 per quarterly meeting to one or more of the presenting organizations, for a total expected commitment of at least $400 per year. Members may contribute more than the minimum and may allocate their contribution across multiple presenting organizations in any proportion they choose.
  • Joining. New members may join at any time by attending a meeting or contacting the Leadership Board. There are no dues or fees payable to the Group itself.
  • Departure. Any member may leave the Group at any time without obligation, simply by notifying the Leadership Board or ceasing to participate.
  • Good Standing. A member is considered in good standing if they have contributed to at least three of the previous four quarterly meetings. Members not in good standing may not nominate charities but may still attend meetings.
Note: Many members contribute without attending meetings, relying on the post-meeting summary to decide their allocation. Meeting attendance is encouraged but not required to remain in good standing.

Article III — Meetings

  • Frequency. The Group meets four times per year, approximately once per quarter. One meeting per year shall be designated as the Annual Meeting for the purpose of electing the Leadership Board. Meetings are typically held at locations in the Wood River Valley.
  • Virtual Option. Whenever possible, a virtual option will be made available for members who cannot attend in person. Virtual attendees count toward quorum and may participate fully in any elections or amendment votes.
  • Duration. Meetings are designed to last approximately one hour.
  • Summer Social. The summer meeting shall be followed by a social gathering to which spouses and significant others are encouraged to attend.
  • Notice. The Communications Coordinator will provide at least fourteen days' advance notice of meeting dates, times, and locations to all members via email and the Group's website. Notice shall include the agenda, the names of the nominated charities, and for the Annual Meeting, the names of any candidates for the Leadership Board.
  • Quorum. No formal quorum is required for charity presentations. For elections and amendments, a quorum of at least twenty-five percent of members in good standing is required.

Article IV — Nomination and Selection of Recipients

  • Eligible Recipients. Any nonprofit organization that is recognized as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code and that serves the Wood River Valley community is eligible to be nominated.
  • Nomination Process. Any member in good standing may nominate a charity by completing and submitting a Nomination Form to the Leadership Board prior to the meeting deadline. The Leadership Board will select three nominees to present at each meeting.
  • Equal Treatment. All three presenting organizations shall receive identical treatment regarding presentation order (which shall be rotated or randomized), presentation time, and prominence in all communications and materials. No presenting organization shall be designated as preferred or primary.
  • Presentations. Each nominated organization (or its nominating member) will have ten minutes to present the organization's mission, the community it serves, why its work is valuable, and why it needs support. This time includes any questions from members. A specific project funding ask is not required — the presentation should help members understand the organization and its impact.
  • Member-Directed Allocation. Following presentations, each member decides how to allocate their minimum $100 contribution across the three presenting organizations. Members may give their entire contribution to one organization, split it equally among all three, or allocate it in any proportion they choose. There is no voting and no "winning" organization.
  • Previous Recipients. An organization that has presented may not be nominated again for a period of eighteen months following its presentation, unless the Leadership Board determines that special circumstances warrant an exception.
  • Absent Members. Members who cannot attend a meeting are still expected to contribute a minimum of $100 to one or more of the presenting organizations. The Communications Coordinator will notify absent members of the three presenting organizations, along with payment information for each, following the meeting.

Article V — Contributions

  • Amount. The minimum contribution is $100 per member per quarterly meeting. Members are encouraged to give more if they are able.
  • Direct Payment. All contributions are made directly by each member to the presenting organizations. No funds pass through the Group at any time.
  • Payment Methods. Members may contribute by any method accepted by the recipient organizations, including:
    • Personal check (mailed or hand-delivered)
    • Online donation through the organization's website
    • ACH bank transfer or bill pay
    • Donor Advised Fund (DAF) grant
    • Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD) from an IRA (for members age 70½+)
    • Peer-to-peer payment services such as Venmo, Zelle, or PayPal
    The Communications Coordinator will include each presenting organization's available payment options and relevant account details in the post-meeting notification to all members.
  • Contribution Verification. Following each quarterly meeting, the Leadership Board may contact each presenting organization to confirm the approximate number and total of contributions received. This is for informational purposes only and does not create an obligation for any individual member.
  • Tax Deductibility. Because contributions are made directly to qualifying 501(c)(3) organizations, each member may claim their own charitable tax deduction. The Group does not provide tax receipts; members should obtain receipts directly from the recipient organizations. Members using digital payment methods should ensure their payment is clearly identified as a charitable donation for their records.
  • Voluntary Nature. Contributions are voluntary. No member shall be subject to legal action for failing to contribute. However, consistent non-participation may affect a member's good standing status under Article II.

Article VI — Leadership Board

Composition and Role

  • Role. The Leadership Board is responsible for organizing meetings, receiving and reviewing nominations, collecting payment information from presenting organizations, communicating with members, maintaining the Group's website and email communications, and ensuring the smooth operation and continuity of the Group.
  • Composition. The Leadership Board shall consist of at least three and no more than seven members, including a Chair, a Vice Chair, and a Communications Coordinator. Board members serve on a volunteer basis.
  • Authority. The Leadership Board has authority over administrative and logistical matters but may not change the fundamental operating principles of this Agreement without a vote of the membership as described in Article X.
  • Leading by Example. Leadership Board members are expected to lead by example, including attending meetings regularly, contributing promptly, and encouraging participation.

Elections

  • Annual Election. Leadership Board members are elected by the membership at the Annual Meeting. Any member in good standing may nominate themselves or another willing member in good standing. The Leadership Board shall solicit nominations at least fourteen days before the Annual Meeting.
  • Terms. Leadership Board members serve staggered three-year terms, with approximately one-third of the seats up for election each year. Initial terms shall be staggered so that some members serve an initial one-year or two-year term to establish the rotation.
  • Term Limits. No individual shall serve more than three consecutive three-year terms on the Leadership Board (nine years total), though they may serve again after a one-year absence from the Board.
  • Voting Method. If the number of candidates exceeds the number of open seats, election shall be by written or electronic ballot. Each member present may vote for as many candidates as there are open seats. The candidates receiving the most votes shall be elected.

Officers

  • Chair. The Leadership Board shall elect a Chair from among its own members. The Chair convenes and facilitates meetings, serves as the primary point of contact for the Group, and oversees day-to-day operations.
  • Vice Chair. The Leadership Board shall elect a Vice Chair from among its own members. The Vice Chair assists the Chair and assumes the Chair's responsibilities in the Chair's absence or incapacity.
  • Communications Coordinator. The Leadership Board shall designate a Communications Coordinator from among its own members. The Communications Coordinator is responsible for maintaining the Group's website, managing the member email list, sending meeting announcements and post-meeting notifications (including the presenting organizations' names and payment instructions), and updating all public-facing information. The Communications Coordinator should maintain written documentation of all account credentials and procedures sufficient to allow a successor to assume the role without disruption.
  • Officer Terms. The Chair, Vice Chair, and Communications Coordinator serve one-year terms, renewable by vote of the Leadership Board, and may serve as officers for no more than four consecutive years in the same role.

Vacancies and Succession

  • Board Vacancies. If a Leadership Board member resigns or is unable to serve, the remaining Board members may appoint a replacement to serve until the next Annual Meeting, at which time the seat will be filled by election for the remainder of the original term.
  • Chair Vacancy. If the Chair resigns, becomes incapacitated, or is otherwise unable to serve, the Vice Chair shall immediately assume the role of Acting Chair and serve in that capacity until the Leadership Board elects a new Chair, which shall occur within thirty days.
  • Emergency Succession. If both the Chair and Vice Chair are unable to serve, the longest-serving member of the Leadership Board shall convene a special meeting of the Board within thirty days to elect new officers.

Article VII — Founding Chair Transition

  • Recognition of Founder. Marty Lyon founded 100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley in 2013 and has served continuously as its organizer and leader. In recognition of his foundational role and ongoing contributions, Marty shall hold the title of Founding Chair for as long as he wishes to serve in that capacity.
  • Phased Transition. The transition from Founding Chair to the elected leadership structure described in Article VI shall occur at Marty's pace and according to his preferences. During any transition period, Marty may delegate specific responsibilities to the elected Leadership Board while retaining the Founding Chair title and such advisory or operational roles as he chooses.
  • Founding Chair Emeritus. Upon stepping back from active leadership, Marty shall be invited to serve as Founding Chair Emeritus permanently. In this role, he may attend meetings, provide advice to the Leadership Board, and remain connected to the Group's work without operational responsibilities.
  • Institutional Knowledge. During any transition, the Leadership Board and Marty shall work together to document institutional knowledge, historical context, key relationships, and operational procedures to ensure continuity.

Article VIII — Communications

Website

  • Group Website. The Group shall maintain a website at www.100menwhocarewoodrivervalley.com (or such other address as the Leadership Board may determine). The website shall display current meeting dates and locations, information about how the Group works, a list of past presenting organizations, the Nomination Form, Leadership Board contact information, and any other information the Leadership Board deems useful to members and the public.
  • Website Management. The Communications Coordinator is responsible for keeping the website current. At a minimum, the website shall be updated after each quarterly meeting to reflect the most recent presenting organizations and the next scheduled meeting date.

Member Communications

  • Meeting Announcements. At least fourteen days before each quarterly meeting, the Communications Coordinator shall send an email to all members containing the date, time, and location of the meeting (including virtual access information if applicable); the names and brief descriptions of the three presenting charities; and for the Annual Meeting, the names of Leadership Board candidates.
  • Post-Meeting Notification. Within forty-eight hours after each quarterly meeting, the Communications Coordinator shall send an email to all members containing the names of the three presenting charities, brief descriptions of each organization's mission and how funds will be used, available payment methods for each (including online donation link, Zelle information, Venmo profile, ACH details, DAF grant instructions, QCD information, and mailing address for checks as applicable), a reminder to include "100MWC" or "100 Men Who Care" in the payment memo, and a reminder to request a tax receipt if one is not provided automatically.
  • Meeting Reminders. The Communications Coordinator may send a reminder email approximately three to five days before each meeting.
  • Payment Follow-Up. Approximately two weeks after each meeting, the Communications Coordinator may send a follow-up email reminding members who have not yet contributed to do so.
  • Communication Platform. The Group's primary communication tool is email. The Leadership Board may also use the website, text messaging, or other channels as supplements, but email shall remain the official means of providing meeting notice and post-meeting payment instructions.

Article IX — Leadership Board Member Responsibilities and Liability

Responsibilities

Service on the Leadership Board is a volunteer commitment. By accepting a seat on the Board, a member agrees to the following responsibilities:

  • Attend and participate. Board members should attend all quarterly meetings and any additional Board meetings. If a Board member cannot attend, they should notify the Chair in advance.
  • Act in good faith. Board members should make decisions that serve the Group's charitable mission, not their personal interests. This includes being fair and impartial in reviewing charity nominations.
  • Fulfill your role. If you hold an officer position (Chair, Vice Chair, or Communications Coordinator), carry out the duties described in Article VI. If you cannot continue to do so, notify the Board promptly so a replacement can be arranged.
  • Maintain confidentiality. Board members should not share members' personal contact information outside the Group without permission, and should handle any sensitive nomination or membership discussions with discretion.
  • Support transitions. When leaving the Board, cooperate fully with the transition process described in Appendix A, including transferring all credentials, files, and institutional knowledge to your successor.
  • Lead by example. Board members should contribute their minimum $100 to each quarterly meeting's presenting organizations and encourage participation by other members.

What Board Service Does Not Require

Board members are not expected to:

  • Contribute more money than any other member
  • Have any particular professional or legal expertise
  • Spend significant time outside of meetings (a few hours per quarter is typical)
  • Take on any financial risk or personal liability
  • Sign contracts, manage money, or make financial commitments on behalf of the Group

Liability

  • No Group Liability. The Group is not a legal entity and assumes no liability. The Group does not enter into contracts, employ staff, or hold assets or funds.
  • No Personal Financial Exposure. Because the Group holds no money, enters no contracts, and employs no one, Leadership Board members have no financial exposure. There are no debts to guarantee, no employees to manage, and no funds to account for.
  • Individual Responsibility for Contributions. Each member is individually responsible for their own charitable contributions and tax reporting. The Leadership Board has no responsibility for any individual member's donations or tax obligations.
  • Volunteer Protection. Leadership Board members and other volunteers act in good faith on behalf of the Group and shall not be personally liable for actions taken in their volunteer capacity, to the extent permitted by Idaho law. Idaho's Volunteer Immunity Act (Idaho Code § 6-1605) provides that volunteers of nonprofit organizations are generally not liable for acts or omissions performed in good faith within the scope of their volunteer duties, provided their conduct does not constitute willful or wanton misconduct.
  • No Fiduciary Duty Over Funds. Because the Group holds no funds, Leadership Board members owe no fiduciary duty with respect to money. Their duty is limited to acting honestly and in good faith in the administration of the Group's meetings and communications.
In plain language: Serving on the Leadership Board means helping to organize four meetings a year and keeping members informed. You are not signing up for any legal or financial obligation. The most important thing you bring is your time, good judgment, and willingness to keep the Group running smoothly.

Article X — Amendments

This Founding Agreement may be amended by a two-thirds vote of members present at any quarterly meeting at which a quorum is present, provided that notice of the proposed amendment has been given to all members at least fourteen days in advance of the meeting.

Article XI — Dissolution

The Group may be dissolved by a two-thirds vote of all members in good standing. Because the Group holds no assets, dissolution requires no distribution of property. The Leadership Board shall notify all members and any pending presenting organizations of the dissolution.
