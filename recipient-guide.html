Recipient Organization Guide

100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley
For Presenting Nonprofits

Congratulations! Your organization has been selected to present at a 100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley quarterly meeting. This guide will help you understand what to expect, how to prepare your presentation, and how to receive contributions from our members.

About 100 Men Who Care

100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley is a volunteer-driven charitable giving circle founded in 2013. Our members are individuals committed to supporting local nonprofits serving the Wood River Valley community.

$757,000+
Contributed to local charities since 2013

How It Works

  • We meet once per quarter (four times per year)
  • Three organizations present at each meeting (including yours)
  • Each organization gets 10 minutes to present (including Q&A)
  • There is no voting — each member decides how to allocate their contribution across all three presenting organizations
  • Members contribute a minimum of $100 per quarter, allocated among the three organizations as they choose
  • All contributions go directly to you — no money passes through the Group
Important: All three presenting organizations receive equal treatment in all communications, on the payment page, and during the meeting. Presentation order is rotated or randomized. No organization is designated as preferred or primary.

What to Expect: Timeline

When You're Selected (~2 Weeks Before Meeting)

  • You'll receive notification from the Communications Coordinator
  • You'll be asked to complete a Payment Info Form (see next section)
  • Deadline: Payment info must be submitted at least 1 week before the meeting

1 Week Before Meeting

  • Your organization appears on the payment page alongside the other two presenters
  • Some members may contribute before the meeting

Day of Meeting

  • You (or your representative) present for 10 minutes
  • Members can access payment page via QR code during presentations
  • Many members contribute during or immediately after the meeting

Within 48 Hours After Meeting

  • All members receive email with your payment details
  • You'll receive a "matching list" for any DAF/QCD gifts (institutional payments)

Following 2–4 Weeks

  • Contributions arrive from members via various payment methods
  • Send personalized acknowledgment to each donor
  • Communications Coordinator will contact you to confirm participation and approximate total

What is DAF Direct?

DAF Direct is a free widget that lets donors grant from their Donor Advised Funds (at Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, or Vanguard Charitable) directly through your payment page. If you're not registered yet, it takes about 30–60 minutes one time. Learn more at:

  • Fidelity Charitable DAF Direct
  • Schwab Charitable DAF Direct

Preparing Your Presentation

Format

  • Time: 10 minutes total (including Q&A)
  • Setting: In-person meeting with virtual attendees possible
  • Audience: ~30–100 members (attendance varies)
  • Visuals: Optional (slides, photos, brief video welcome)

What to Cover

Your presentation should help members understand:

  1. Your mission — What does your organization do?
  2. Community served — Who benefits from your work?
  3. Why your work matters — What problem are you solving?
  4. Impact — What difference do you make?
  5. Why you need support — How will unrestricted funds help?
✓ A specific project ask is NOT required. Unrestricted operating support is perfectly acceptable. Many members appreciate understanding your overall mission rather than being asked to fund a narrow project.

Presentation Tips

DO:

  • ✓ Tell stories — specific examples resonate more than statistics
  • ✓ Explain your unique value — what makes your approach special?
  • ✓ Be authentic — members appreciate genuine passion
  • ✓ Connect to the local community — members care about the Wood River Valley
  • ✓ Leave time for questions (2–3 minutes recommended)

DON'T:

  • ✗ Go over time — you'll be cut off at 10 minutes
  • ✗ Read from slides — engage with the audience
  • ✗ Use jargon — speak in plain language
  • ✗ Assume prior knowledge — some members may be unfamiliar with your work
💡 Example Structure (10 minutes):
  • Opening (1 min): Who you are, quick story or statistic
  • Mission & Community Served (2 min): What you do and who benefits
  • Impact & Why It Matters (3 min): Real examples of your work
  • Why You Need Support (2 min): How funds will be used
  • Q&A (2 min): Member questions

Receiving Contributions

How Members Will Pay

Members contribute directly to your organization using the payment methods you've provided. Common methods include:

Payment Method How It Arrives Fees?
Check Mailed or hand-delivered to your address None
Zelle Direct to your bank account None
ACH / Bill Pay Direct to your bank account None
DAF (Donor Advised Fund) Check or ACH from "Fidelity Charitable", "Schwab Charitable", etc. None
QCD (IRA Distribution) Check from member's IRA custodian (Schwab, Fidelity, Vanguard, etc.) None

Venmo / PayPal To your organization account ~2%

Identifying 100MWC Gifts

Members are instructed to include "100MWC" or "100 Men Who Care" in all payment memos/notes. Look for this identifier when gifts arrive.

⚠️ Matching List for DAF/QCD Gifts: When members use Donor Advised Funds or Qualified Charitable Distributions, the payment arrives from an institutional account (like "Fidelity Charitable" or "Charles Schwab") rather than the member's personal name. The Communications Coordinator will send you a matching list that connects these institutional gifts back to individual members. Use this list to send personalized acknowledgments.

Example Matching List

EXPECTED INSTITUTIONAL GIFTS: • John Smith - via Fidelity Charitable - sent approx. Feb 15 Email: john@example.com • Jane Doe - via Schwab Charitable - sent approx. Feb 16 Email: jane@example.com • Bob Jones - via Vanguard (QCD) - sent approx. Feb 18 Email: bob@example.com

How to use it: When you receive a grant from "Fidelity Charitable" around February 15, check the matching list — that's John Smith's contribution. Send your acknowledgment letter to john@example.com.

Donor Acknowledgment: Critical Next Step

Please send personalized acknowledgment letters or emails to every donor. This is important for several reasons:

  • Members need written acknowledgment for tax deduction purposes (IRS requirement for gifts $250+)
  • Personalized thank-yous strengthen the donor relationship
  • Members appreciate knowing their gift was received
  • Strong stewardship encourages members to support you again if you present in the future

What to Include in Acknowledgments

  • Thank you for the contribution
  • Amount received
  • Date received
  • Statement: "No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution" (required for tax purposes)
  • Your 501(c)(3) status and EIN
  • How the gift will be used (optional but appreciated)

Sample Acknowledgment Letter

[Your Letterhead] [Date] Dear [Member Name], Thank you for your generous contribution of $[amount] to [Your Organization] through 100 Men Who Care Wood River Valley. We received your gift on [date] via [payment method]. Your support helps us [brief impact statement — one sentence about how funds will be used or what they enable]. [Your Organization] is a 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization (EIN: XX-XXXXXXX). No goods or services were provided in exchange for this contribution. We are deeply grateful for your partnership in our mission to [mission]. Sincerely, [Executive Director Name] [Title]

Follow-Up with Communications Coordinator

3–4 weeks after the meeting, the Communications Coordinator will contact you to ask:

  • How many individual gifts did you receive from 100MWC members?
  • What was the approximate total? (If you're comfortable sharing)
  • Any feedback about the experience?

This information helps the Group:

  • Track overall impact and participation
  • Update the website with meeting results
  • Celebrate the collective generosity of members
  • Improve the process for future presenting organizations

Your candid feedback is welcome — if something didn't work well or could be improved, please let us know.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we present again?

Yes! However, there's an 18-month cooldown period before you can be nominated again. This ensures a variety of organizations have the opportunity to present.

How much should we expect to receive?

It varies based on member participation and how members allocate their contributions across the three presenting organizations. With ~100 members each contributing a minimum of $100 split among three organizations, a typical range might be $1,000–$7,000 per organization, but this is not guaranteed.

What if we can't present in person?

You may send a representative from your organization, or arrange with the Communications Coordinator to present virtually if the meeting has a virtual option.

Do we need to prepare a written proposal?

No. The member who nominated your organization will have completed a nomination form. You only need to prepare your 10-minute oral presentation.

Can we bring handouts or promotional materials?

Yes, but keep it simple. A one-page flyer or business card is sufficient. Members will receive your payment details via email after the meeting.

What if we don't have Zelle or online donation set up?

At minimum, you can accept checks. However, we strongly encourage setting up at least one fee-free electronic payment method (Zelle is easiest — just contact your bank). Many members prefer not to mail checks, and electronic options increase participation.
