100 Men Who Care

Payment Simplification: A Jobs-to-be-Done Research Proposal

⚠️ This is a Hypothesis Document

The problems, solutions, and expected outcomes described below are assumptions that require validation through design thinking research with members, coordinators, charities, and community foundation partners.

Executive Summary

Context: 100 Men Who Care has grown into a powerful force for local philanthropy, raising $125,000 in the most recent year ($757,000+ cumulative over 12 years). The model works: three charities present each quarter, members decide their own allocation, and write checks to Marty (the coordinator) who hand-delivers them to the recipients.

$125,000 Raised in most recent year
$757,000+ Cumulative over 12 years
Unknown Actual follow-through rate (to be measured)

The Opportunity: We believe there are potential opportunities to increase charitable impact by:

  • Reducing "out of sight, out of mind" forgetting through immediate payment options
  • Making tax-advantaged giving (QCD/DAF) simpler so members give more using pre-tax dollars
  • Reducing administrative burden on Marty and recipient charity staff
  • Improving impact tracking and reporting

Proposed Approach: Before building anything, conduct design thinking research with all stakeholders (members, Marty, charity Executive Directors, Spur Foundation, Idaho Community Foundation) to validate these assumptions using the Jobs-to-be-Done framework.

If validated, implement a hybrid solution: Automate DAF (using free DAF Direct widget), provide simple instructions + templates for QCD, and create a voluntary tracking mechanism.

Current Model: What's Working

The 100MWC Process (Correct Model)

It's important to clarify the actual model before identifying problems:

  1. Three charities present at each quarterly meeting (no voting)
  2. Each member decides individually which charity/charities to support
  3. Members can allocate flexibly — $100 to one, $50 to two, $33 to each, etc.
  4. Members write checks and send to Marty (coordinator)
  5. Marty hand-delivers all checks to the three charities
  6. Follow-through is strong — Marty knows exactly how much each charity receives

What's Working Exceptionally Well

  • Strong growth trajectory — $125K in most recent year shows increasing engagement
  • Simple, replicable model — minimal administrative overhead
  • Reliable delivery system — Marty's hand-delivery ensures money reaches charities
  • Charity-level tracking — group knows exactly how much each charity received (total amounts)
  • Member engagement — anecdotally strong (to be validated in research: do members value the three-charity presentation and allocation flexibility?)
  • Community recognition — strong brand in Wood River Valley

Important Context: The $100 figure often referenced is the minimum expectation for membership. Many active members give more than $100 per quarter, especially those who split allocations across multiple charities or who give larger amounts to causes they're passionate about. Actual giving patterns and amounts are currently unknown and should be explored in research.

Key Insight: The current system is not broken. This proposal is about exploring whether we can make a good system even better by:

  • Understanding actual participation rates (currently unknown)
  • Removing friction to increase follow-through
  • Enabling more generous giving through tax-advantaged methods

Observed Problems (To Be Validated)

These are hypotheses based on anecdotal feedback. Design research will validate whether these are real pain points and how significant they are.

Problem 1: "Out of Sight, Out of Mind"

Observation: Some members leave the meeting with genuine intention to give but forget in the days/weeks afterward. Life intervenes between writing the check and mailing it to Marty.

To validate: Interview members about their actual donation process and timing. How many days between meeting and mailing check? Do they ever forget?

Problem 2: Not Using Tax-Advantaged Sources

Observation: Members who have Donor Advised Funds (DAF) or Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCD) available give less than they could because:

  • They don't realize QCD/DAF can be used for 100MWC
  • The process seems complicated (researching charity details, navigating custodian portals)
  • They default to writing a $100 check from their regular account
  • A member who could give $200 via QCD (pre-tax) gives $100 via check instead

To validate: How many members have DAFs or are QCD-eligible (age 70½+)? Have they ever used these for 100MWC? What stopped them?

Problem 3: Unnecessary Work for Marty

Observation: Collecting 80-100 checks quarterly, tracking them, and hand-delivering to three charities requires significant time and logistics.

To validate: Interview Marty. How much time does this take? What are the friction points? What would make this easier?

Problem 4: Unnecessary Work for Charity Executive Directors

Observation: Small charity staffs must:

  • Process 30-50 individual checks (deposit, record, acknowledge each)
  • Match institutional checks (DAF grants, QCD custodian checks) to individual donors without clear identification
  • Send individual acknowledgment letters

To validate: Interview recent recipient charity Executive Directors. How long does it take to process 100MWC gifts? What's the hardest part? Have they received unidentifiable institutional checks?

Problem 5: No Visibility into Participation Rates

Observation: While Marty knows how much each charity received (total dollars), the group doesn't know:

  • Participation rate: What % of attendees actually donated?
  • Who gave vs. who didn't: Can't send gentle reminders to non-participants
  • Payment method breakdown: How many used DAF/QCD vs. check vs. Zelle?
  • Per-charity allocation patterns: How did members split their giving across the three charities?
  • Real-time status: During collection period, can't see how many have completed vs. pending

Impact: Can't answer "What % of members follow through?" Can't send targeted reminders. Limited data for recruiting and impact storytelling.

To validate: Interview Marty and leadership board. What participation data would be useful? What questions do prospects ask that we can't answer?

Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

Before designing solutions, we need to understand the jobs each stakeholder is trying to accomplish. The JTBD framework focuses on: When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome].

Persona 1: The Member (Donor)

Profile: 50-75 years old, community-minded, likely has IRA with RMD and/or DAF. Values efficiency, impact, and tax optimization.

"I left the meeting inspired. I want to donate right away while I remember, but I also want to do it the smart way tax-wise. I just don't want it to be a research project."

When: I leave the quarterly meeting inspired to give

I want to: Complete my donation immediately while the energy is fresh

So I can: Fulfill my commitment without it slipping through the cracks or getting deprioritized

Current barrier: Must wait until I get home, find checkbook, look up address, write check, find envelope/stamp, mail to Marty. Takes days. Easy to forget.

When: I'm deciding how much to give and which method to use

I want to: Understand my tax-advantaged options (QCD/DAF) without doing research

So I can: Be more generous using pre-tax dollars instead of limiting myself to $100 from my checking account

Current barrier: Don't know if I can use my DAF/QCD for this. Don't want to spend 30 minutes looking up charity details and navigating my custodian's portal. Default to regular check.

When: I've made my donation

I want to: Know it was received and will be acknowledged for tax purposes

So I can: Have peace of mind and proper documentation for my tax return

Current barrier: No confirmation mechanism. Hope Marty got my check. Wait weeks for charity acknowledgment letter (if it arrives).

When: I'm allocating across three charities

I want to: Easily split my gift (e.g., $50/$30/$20)

So I can: Support multiple causes that resonated with me

Current barrier: Must write three separate checks with three different addresses. Tedious.

Research Questions for Members:

  • Walk me through your process from the meeting to sending your donation. How long does it take? What's hardest?
  • Do you have a Donor Advised Fund? Have you ever used it for 100MWC? Why or why not?
  • Are you age 70½+ and QCD-eligible? Have you ever used QCD for 100MWC? What stopped you?
  • Have you ever intended to donate but forgot? What happened?
  • If you could donate from your phone while sitting at dinner after the meeting, would you? What would you need to see/know?

Persona 2: The Coordinator (Marty)

Profile: Volunteer on leadership board, organized, cares deeply about the group's continuity and impact. Willing to spend time but wants efficient systems.

"I want the group to run smoothly and money to flow to charities quickly. But I'm a volunteer—I can't chase 100 people or spend hours on logistics every quarter."

When: Managing donations after the quarterly meeting

I want to: Ensure money flows to all three charities efficiently

So I can: Fulfill my role without it becoming a second job

Current barrier: Collect checks over weeks via mail. Track manually. Coordinate delivery to three different charities. Time-intensive.

When: Communicating payment options to members

I want to: Provide complete, clear information for all payment methods

So I can: Get zero follow-up questions and know members have what they need

Current barrier: Send basic email with charity names. Don't have Zelle/Venmo/ACH details. Don't explain QCD/DAF options. Members ask questions or figure it out themselves.

When: Reporting impact to the board and community

I want to: Provide accurate, detailed data on participation and giving

So I can: Tell a compelling story for recruiting new members and community relations

Current barrier: Know totals per charity but not: participation rate, payment method breakdown, allocation patterns, trends over time.

When: Preparing for the next quarter

I want to: Follow a documented, repeatable process

So I can: Ensure consistency and make handoff to my successor easy

Current barrier: Process is mostly tribal knowledge. Each quarter requires reinventing workflows.

Research Questions for Marty:

  • Walk me through your workflow each quarter. Where does time go? What's most tedious?
  • What questions do members ask you about payment? What info do they need that you don't currently provide?
  • How do you currently track who has donated and how much? What data do you wish you had?
  • If you could wave a magic wand and simplify one part of this, what would it be?
  • How would you feel about electronic payments (Zelle, ACH, etc.) instead of physical checks?

Persona 3: The Executive Director (Recipient Charity)

Profile: Executive Director (ED) of a small/midsize local 501(c)(3). Staff of 1-15. Uses donor management system or spreadsheet. Accustomed to community gifts but not bulk QCD/DAF.

"Being selected by 100 Men is wonderful! But then I get 30-50 checks over three weeks, some from people I've never heard of, and I need to process each one, match the weird institutional checks to actual donors, and send acknowledgments. It's a lot for our small team."

When: Receiving 100MWC gifts

I want to: Identify each gift and match it to a donor name

So I can: Send personalized acknowledgments and build relationships

Current barrier: Institutional checks from "Fidelity Charitable" or "Schwab Custodian for [member]" arrive with no context. Don't know which 100MWC member sent it. Can't send personal thank-you.

When: Receiving 100MWC gifts

I want to: Get each donor's email address alongside their name

So I can: Follow up electronically and keep them informed about our organization's progress

Current barrier: Even when a matching list arrives with donor names, it contains no contact information. A paper acknowledgment letter can be sent, but there is no channel for ongoing electronic follow-up. These are warm donors who have already chosen to give — the opportunity to convert them into long-term supporters through updates, impact stories, and future appeals is lost because we have no email address.

When: Processing the gift batch

I want to: Handle deposits, data entry, and acknowledgments efficiently

So I can: Not overwhelm my small team with administrative work

Current barrier: 30-50 individual checks arrive over weeks. Each must be deposited, entered into donor database, matched to contact info, acknowledgment letter generated. Takes 4-6 hours spread over weeks.

When: Stewarding the relationship with 100MWC

I want to: Thank the group collectively and individual donors personally

So I can: Build long-term relationships and potentially convert 100MWC members into recurring donors

Current barrier: No structured communication channel with the group. Don't have a consolidated donor list. Send general thank-you but miss opportunity for individual stewardship.

Research Questions for Charity Executive Directors (Executive Directors):

  • Walk me through what happens when you're selected by 100MWC. How do gifts arrive? How long does processing take?
  • Have you ever received institutional checks (from DAF sponsors or IRA custodians) that you couldn't identify? What did you do?
  • What would make processing 100MWC gifts easier for your team?
  • If you knew in advance which donors were sending DAF/QCD gifts, how would that help?
  • Do you currently have relationships with Spur Foundation or Idaho Community Foundation?

Proposed Solution: Hybrid Approach

IF design research validates the problems above, we propose a hybrid solution that balances automation with simplicity:

Core Concept: Instructions-First, Automation Where It's Quick and Easy

Create a simple "Contribute Page" (web page or PDF) accessible via QR code and URL, updated each quarter with the three presenting charities. For each charity, display all payment methods with:

  • DAF: Automate with DAF Direct widget (free widget exists, one-click for donor, but charities must register)
  • QCD: Provide clear instructions + downloadable pre-filled templates (can't automate due to varying custodian processes)
  • Other methods: Simple instructions (Zelle account, check address, etc.)
  • Tracking: Optional "I've Donated" button → Google Form

Payment Methods: Automation Where It's Quick and Easy

Decision principle: Automate where free tools exist and setup is straightforward. Use instructions + templates where processes vary or automation is complex.

📋 DAF (Donor Advised Fund)

AUTOMATE

Why automate: Standardized process across major sponsors. Free DAF Direct widget already exists.

National sponsors (Fidelity, Schwab, Vanguard):

  • Embed DAF Direct widget on contribute page
  • Member clicks "Give with DAF" → selects sponsor → logs in → charity pre-filled → one-click confirm
  • Charity registration required: Each of the three charities must register with DAF Direct, provide EIN, customize widget (30-60 min one-time setup per charity)
  • Zero development cost (widget code is free)
Local Foundations

Spur Community Foundation & Idaho Community Foundation:

Provide login links + simple instructions: "Log into your portal → Recommend Grant → Search for [Charity Name] (EIN: XX-XXXXXXX)"

Research whether Spur/ICF can be added to DAF Direct network.

📄 QCD (Qualified Charitable Distribution)

INSTRUCTIONS + TEMPLATES

Why keep simple: Processes vary by custodian. Can't fully automate. Templates solve the research burden.

What to provide:

  • Downloadable instruction letter template: Pre-filled with charity name, address, EIN, amount. Member adds their name, submits to custodian.
  • Downloadable check slip: For IRA checkbook holders (Fidelity, Schwab). Pre-filled details, member transcribes onto IRA check.
  • Custodian-specific instructions: "Fidelity: Fill out one-time form. Schwab: Use IRA checkbook. Vanguard: Click 'Request QCD' button."

💸 Zelle / Venmo / ACH

INSTRUCTIONS

What to display:

  • Zelle: "Send to [email/phone]. Memo: '100MWC Q1 2026'"
  • Venmo: "Send to [@handle]. Note: '100MWC Q1 2026'"
  • ACH: "Routing: XXXXX, Account: XXXXX. Memo: '100MWC'"

Already simple. Just need account details.

✉️ Check (Mail or Hand to Marty)

INSTRUCTIONS + TEMPLATE

What to provide:

  • Mailing address displayed
  • Downloadable check slip: Pre-filled with charity name, address, memo. Member transcribes onto check.
  • Option to hand to Marty at meeting or mail directly

💳 Credit / Debit Card

LINK

What to provide:

Direct link to charity's donation page. Note processing fees (2-4%).

Already automated by charity. We just link to it.

Why DAF Direct Widget?

DAF Direct is a free, widely-adopted widget that major DAF sponsors created specifically for nonprofits. Here's why it's the right solution for DAF:

Feature Details
Cost Free for nonprofits and donors (zero transaction fees)
Supported Sponsors Fidelity Charitable, Schwab Charitable, Vanguard Charitable, and growing
Donor Experience Click "Give with DAF" → select sponsor → log in → charity name/EIN pre-filled → one-click confirm
Nonprofit Registration Required. Charity must register with DAF Direct, provide EIN and organizational info, confirm 501(c)(3) status. Estimated time: 30-60 minutes one-time setup. (Tutorial)
Implementation After registration, customize widget and embed code on contribute page (like embedding a YouTube video). Takes 10-15 minutes.
Grant Processing 2-10 business days (standard DAF timeline)
Donor Identification Charity receives donor name and contact info (unless donor opts for anonymity, which is rare)

This is the "simplification" we're looking for on the DAF side — it's already built, proven, and used by thousands of nonprofits nationwide.

Why Instructions + Templates for QCD?

QCD processes vary significantly by custodian and can't be fully automated like DAF. But we CAN eliminate the research burden:

  • Pre-filled instruction letter: Member downloads PDF with charity name, address, EIN already filled in. Just add their name and submit to custodian via portal or mail.
  • Pre-filled check slip: For members with IRA checkbooks (Fidelity, Schwab), provide a slip they transcribe onto their check. No looking up details.
  • Custodian-specific guidance: "If you have Fidelity, do X. If you have Schwab, do Y." Clear next steps.

This is "good enough" simplification — QCD doesn't need to be one-click, it just needs to not be a research project.

  • Show pre-filled QCD template: "Does this remove the hassle? What's still hard?"

For Spur & Idaho Community Foundation:

  • Approximately how many of your DAF holders are Wood River Valley residents?
  • Do you support (or could you support) the DAF Direct widget integration?
  • What percentage of your DAF holders actively make grants each year?
  • Would you be interested in partnering with 100MWC on educational outreach about DAF giving?
  • Do you have relationships with local charities that 100MWC typically supports?

Expected Research Outcomes

Research should answer:

  1. Problem validation: Are the five problems real and significant, or are they minor edge cases?
  2. DAF/QCD prevalence: How many members actually have DAFs or are QCD-eligible? (If it's only 5%, solution may not be worth effort.)
  3. Local vs. national DAFs: Are most DAFs with Fidelity/Schwab/Vanguard (DAF Direct compatible) or Spur/ICF (need custom approach)?
  4. Solution validation: Would the proposed contribute page + templates actually solve the problems, or are we missing something?
  5. Barriers we haven't considered: What objections or concerns arise that we didn't anticipate?

Decision Point: If research shows problems are minor or DAF/QCD prevalence is very low, we may conclude the current system is optimal and abandon the project. That's a valid outcome.

Only proceed with implementation if research validates significant opportunity.

Hypotheses to Test

These are the core assumptions we're validating through research:

  1. H1: Immediate payment options reduce forgetting

    If members can donate via QR code → contribute page → payment in 30 seconds, follow-through rate will increase because "out of sight, out of mind" window shrinks from days to seconds.

    Validation: Ask members "Would you donate immediately via phone if you could?" Pilot and track "I've Donated" form timestamps.

  2. H2: Simplified DAF/QCD increases gift sizes and usage

    If DAF is one-click (widget) and QCD is pre-filled template, members who currently give $100 via check will give $150-$200 via tax-advantaged methods. Usage of DAF/QCD will rise from ~5% to 20-30% of donations.

    Validation: Ask members "If using your DAF were as easy as credit card, would you use it? How much would you give?" Track method breakdown after implementation.

  3. H3: Electronic payments reduce coordinator workload

    If members use Zelle/DAF/QCD direct instead of checks to Marty, coordinator time spent on collection/delivery drops by 50%+.

    Validation: Interview Marty about current time spent. Pilot and measure time after implementation.

  4. H4: Better identification reduces charity ED workload

    If DAF grants include donor names (via DAF Direct) and QCD templates include member names, charities can match institutional checks to donors without detective work. Processing time drops by 30%+.

    Validation: Interview charity Executive Directors about current processing time and identification issues. Pilot and measure.

  5. H5: "I've Donated" tracking enables better impact storytelling

    If members voluntarily log their donations via Google Form, leadership gains real-time participation data, method breakdown, and trend analysis for recruiting and community relations.

    Validation: Interview leadership about data needs. Pilot and measure adoption of "I've Donated" form.

Implementation Approach (If Validated)

Phase 1: MVP — Instructions + Templates (Low Cost, Fast)

If research validates the opportunity, start with the simplest implementation:

Build (2-4 weeks):

  1. Create contribute page: Simple web page or well-designed PDF with three charities, all payment methods, clear instructions
  2. Embed DAF Direct widget: Add widget code for national DAF sponsors
  3. Create downloadable templates:
    • QCD instruction letter (fillable PDF)
    • QCD check slip (printable PDF)
    • Regular check slip (printable PDF)
  4. Set up "I've Donated" Google Form: Two questions, auto-populates spreadsheet for Marty
  5. Create QR code: Links to contribute page

Cost Estimate:

  • Web development (simple page + widget): 10-15 hours (~$1,000-$1,500 if hiring, $0 if volunteer)
  • Template design (PDFs): 5 hours (~$300-$500 or volunteer)
  • Hosting: ~$10/month
  • Total one-time cost: $1,300-$2,000 (or $0 with volunteers)

Phase 2: Pilot Quarter (Q2 or Q3 2026)

  1. Pre-meeting: All three presenting charities fill out payment info form (Zelle, Venmo, ACH, donation page URL)
  2. Post-meeting: Marty updates contribute page with winning charities (15 min setup)
  3. Email blast: Send templated email with QR code to all attendees
  4. Monitor: Track "I've Donated" form submissions in real-time
  5. 2-week reminder: Send gentle nudge to non-responders (if "I've Donated" adoption is high enough to make this useful)
  6. End of quarter: Collect feedback from members, Marty, and charity Executive Directors

Phase 3: Refine & Scale

  1. Analyze pilot data:
    • Did follow-through rate increase?
    • How many used DAF/QCD? Did gift sizes increase?
    • Did Marty's workload decrease?
    • Did charity processing become easier?
    • What % of members used "I've Donated" form?
  2. Iterate based on feedback: Adjust UI, add missing payment methods, improve email copy, refine templates
  3. Full rollout: If pilot is successful, make contribute page permanent infrastructure
  4. Quarterly review: Track success metrics, report to Leadership Board
  5. Annual impact report: Use real data to tell 100MWC story to community

  • Reduced forgetting: 5-10% more members follow through via immediate payment (+$6K-$12K)
  • Larger tax-advantaged gifts: Members using DAF/QCD give 20-50% more per person (+$9K-$18K)

Both Spur Community Foundation and Idaho Community Foundation should be treated as strategic partners, not just service providers:

Spur Community Foundation

  • Zero fees: 100% of donations available to grant (vs. 0.6% admin fee at national DAFs)

  • Partnership opportunity: Co-host educational event on tax-advantaged giving? Joint marketing?

Idaho Community Foundation

  • Statewide reach: Long track record, manages multiple WRV-specific funds
  • Endowed + non-endowed options: Flexibility for different donor goals
  • Partnership opportunity: Similar to Spur — education, co-marketing, streamlined grant process

Next Steps

This proposal lays out a hypothesis-driven approach to potentially increasing charitable impact for 100 Men Who Care. The current system works well. The question is: can we make it work even better?

We don't know yet. That's why we start with research, not solutions.

How the Proposed Solution Serves Each Persona

For Members (Donors):

  • Job: "Give immediately while energy is fresh" → Solution: QR code in post-meeting email links to contribute page with all payment options instantly accessible on phone
  • Job: "Understand tax-advantaged options without research" → Solution: DAF Direct widget (one-click) + pre-filled QCD templates eliminate the research burden
  • Job: "Be more generous using pre-tax dollars" → Solution: Making DAF/QCD as easy as credit card removes barrier to giving $150-$200 instead of $100
  • Job: "Easily split gift across three charities" → Solution: All three charities displayed equally with same payment options; member controls allocation

For Marty (Coordinator):

  • Job: "Ensure money flows to charities without becoming second job" → Solution: Electronic payments (DAF, Zelle) reduce physical check collection/delivery burden
  • Job: "Provide complete payment information with zero follow-up questions" → Solution: Contribute page lists all methods with clear instructions; one resource answers everything
  • Job: "Follow repeatable process quarter after quarter" → Solution: Standard checklist, same page template, just update charity details (15 min/quarter)

For Charity Executive Directors:

  • Job: "Identify each gift and match to donor name" → Solution: DAF Direct includes donor info; QCD templates include member names; all payment instructions emphasize "100MWC" in memo
  • Job: "Process efficiently without overwhelming small team" → Solution: Better identification = less detective work; concentrated arrival window vs. weeks of trickle
  • Job: "Send personalized acknowledgments" → Solution: Donor identification built into DAF/QCD methods; no more mystery institutional checks

The common thread: Remove friction, provide clarity, make the right thing easy. IF research validates these jobs and solutions, we'll have addressed real needs for all stakeholders.

  2. If yes, recruit research lead: Identify who will conduct stakeholder interviews (could be volunteer, board member, or contractor)
  3. Schedule interviews: 5-8 members, Marty, 2-3 charity Executive Directors, Spur, Idaho Community Foundation (4-6 weeks)
  4. Synthesize findings: Validate or invalidate the five hypotheses
  5. Decision point: Proceed with pilot, modify approach, or conclude current system is optimal
